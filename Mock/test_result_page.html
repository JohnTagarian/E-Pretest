Question 02
cancel Incorrect

Find the derivative of $f(x) = \ln(x^2 + 1)$.

Your Answer

$1 / (x^2 + 1)$

Correct Answer

$2x / (x^2 + 1)$

Explanation

The chain rule must be applied here. Let $u = x^2 + 1$. Then $f(x) = \ln(u)$, and $f'(x) = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$. Since $\frac{du}{dx} = 2x$, the final result is $\frac{2x}{x^2 + 1}$. You forgot to multiply by the derivative of the inner function.

menu_book Reference: Essential Calculus, Section 3.4, Theorem 2

State the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part 1).

Your Answer

Correct Answer

If $f$ is continuous on $[a, b]$, then $g(x) = \int_a^x f(t) dt$ has derivative $g'(x) = f(x)$.

Explanation

This theorem connects differential calculus and integral calculus. It states that differentiation and integration are inverse processes.
